01 What it is

A safety gate between your AI and your money.

You write the rules. PaymentGuard enforces them on every payment — the AI doesn't get a vote.

PaymentGuard lets you set simple rules — who can get paid, how much, and for how long. Every payment your AI tries to make has to pass these rules first. Because the rules run as plain code, the AI can't argue with them, bend them, or skip them.

⌘ The simplest way to picture it

Think of the spending controls on a company card: set once, checked on every purchase, and impossible for the person holding the card to switch off. PaymentGuard does that — for your AI.

03 How it benefits you

The attacks a spending limit never sees.

A number stops the obvious. PaymentGuard stops everything clever — the sneaky drains, the unknown payees, the prompt injections.

An AI can't talk its way past it

Even if a hacker hides instructions inside an email or a web page, the rules don't listen. They're code, not a conversation — there's nothing to persuade.

It catches the sneaky attacks

Not just big payments. It stops money going to people you never approved, and drains hidden across lots of tiny payments that slip past a simple limit.

You decide ahead of time

You choose who can be paid, how much, and until when. Everything has an expiry date, and you can cancel any rule the moment you change your mind.

A receipt for every decision

Every yes and no is written to a log that can't be secretly edited. You can always prove exactly what happened, and spot it if anyone tries to tamper with it.

About

The safest place for your money is one that never holds it.

PaymentGuard never holds or touches your money. It only answers one question — is this payment allowed? — and lets your real card, bank, or crypto do the actual moving. Nothing to hack, nothing to run off with.

Developers can add it to an AI assistant like Claude or Cursor in one line, or drop it into their own app. The engine running the live demo above is the exact same code anyone can install — and it's completely free and open source.

FAQ

Quick answers.

Does PaymentGuard ever hold my money? +
No — it never touches your money. It only says yes or no to a payment. The money itself moves through whatever you already use: your card, your bank, or crypto.
Can a hacked AI get around the rules? +
No. The rules run as plain code, completely separate from the AI. A hacker can fool the assistant with a hidden message — but they can't talk the rules into letting a payment through.
What can I pay with? +
Anything. PaymentGuard doesn't move money itself, so it works on top of cards, bank transfers, or crypto — whatever you already use.
How do I add it? +
One line. To use it with an AI assistant:
npx -y payment-guard
Or to use it inside your own app:
npm install payment-guard
Is it really free? +
Yes — completely free and open source under the MIT license. The engine running the demo on this page is the exact code you'd install.
